Pixelated, yes it's very typical for our national forest system to swap land with private land owners. They typically do it in checkerboard fashion, every other square mile (land survey plots). You can tell the private land from public land as the public land is usually more forested.

The land around it is industrial forest land either forested or deforested. The checkerboard nature at least keeps public access open for the most part.
