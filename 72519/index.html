If you have checked that the data is correct in OpenStreetMap and that the incorrect information isn't also present then there is not much you can do from the OpenStreetMap end of things.

Those using data from OpenStreetMap take updates from the database at their own rate. Some update hourly and others never update at all. If the OSM data has been corrected your best chance is to contact Instagram and see if they can flag your area or update (or whatever their internal system is).
