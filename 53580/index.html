OpenStreetMap != Google.

Like I said above, OpenStreetMap is not a "service". OpenStreetMap has a whole bunch of data that you can download and do what you want with http://planet.osm.org/ , subject to what it says at http://www.openstreetmap.org/copyright . Some imagery providers have made imagery available for use by OpenStreetMap, but (in the case of Bing) that's explicitly for use in the editors - see https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Bing_Maps .

There are third-party imagery services available - I'd suggest you talk to one of those and discuss licensing.
