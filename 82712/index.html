The TEN-T network, and it's associated corridors, is not something that is likely to be mapped on OSM. It is more a theoretical construct for planning investment in transport infrastructure than a road or rail network clearly signed on the ground. In general OSM works to an 'on-the-ground' principle which means that multiple observers are likely to recognise a particular feature, and TEN-T does not fulfil this criterion.
