First, thank you for correcting the location of the church in Openstreetmap. We rely on people with on the ground knowledge to make sure the data are correct! The data in OSM are used by many companies to make their maps, including Facebook. The process of turning the data into maps is called rendering and different companies render at different frequencies. I don't know what Facebook's policy is, but apparently they do not render as frequently as does the map on the Openstreetmap. As the other commenter said, you have done everything correct on the OSM database, but Facebook is the problem.

Best of luck with that corporate bureaucracy!
