Looking at http://www.openstreetmap.org/browse/changeset/10960671 , it looks like both edits are in the same changeset. Potlatch 2 will auto-close a changeset after an hour; if you edit again within this period of time it'll go in the same changeset. If you look at http://www.openstreetmap.org/browse/node/1672598060/history , you can see the node added at 21:12, and then edited to remove the name 37 minutes later.

(12 Mar '12, 23:04) SomeoneElse ♦

(and to try and answer the actual question) http://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Potlatch/Primer#Undoing describes the "undo" options in Potlatch - that'll work while you're still editing.

To find a particular node that you've edited, go to "my edits" (in your case http://www.openstreetmap.org/user/HellaHH/edits), and open the most recent or current changeset. You can click on a node in there (for example 1672598060, v2) and then select edit, and it'll take you into the editor with that node selected.
