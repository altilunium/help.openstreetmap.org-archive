That is what our wiki says:

  • "The way used should point in the direction of water flow." (Waterways)
  • "By convention the direction of the way should be downstream" (Key:waterway)

Apparently the waterway creators in your example did not follow the convention. That's all.

By the way, if you are using the editor Potlatch2 (which I assume due to your last edit's meta info) you can change a way's directon with the shortcut key V in case you want to change it. If you would use JOSM use the reverse way tool (shortcut key R) in the tools menu similarly. For iD click a way and use the reverse tool in the pop-up toolbox (shortcut V). Have a look in the way's history first (to find any reason for the uncommon way direction).
