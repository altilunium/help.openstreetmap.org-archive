I’ve used a Garmin 62s for the last 3 years for surveying. It’s perfectly adequate for such use. I load up routes from a PC and follow these rather than just using it to check my location in relation to a paper map. It seems that this is no longer made and has been replaced by the 64 series so you may well get a good deal on this model at the moment. If you’re a walker, OSM coverage of footpaths can be patchy so getting the 1:50 OS UK map might be a good idea, although the detail of the 1:25 is really the "gold standard" when it comes to walking. For cycling OSM maps are likely to be superior.

I don’t think Satmap will allow you to load up your own maps. You can buy OSM based cycle maps for the device and it seems its planning software will allow you to use OSM.
