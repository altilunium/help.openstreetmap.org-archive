You can keep your software closed without problems, as cartinus said. But there is a difference in the two licenses.

For the CC-BY-SA license, you need to release any derived work again under CC-BY-SA. That means, if you render your own style of tiles, those have to be released under CC-BY-SA, so people are allowed to copy your style. You don't have to open-source the software though. It's not because a photo is licensed under CC-BY-SA that it can't be edited with the closed photoshop.

For the ODBL license, you can also copyright your style (off coarse, up to the limit where copyrights go, it has to be original when you want to copyright it, not based on some existing style). For the ODBL, only the data has to be open.

On the other hand, copyrighting of tiles is quite vague, as people can always get "inspired" by other rendering, and use that inspiration for their own tiles. You can use that for your own tiles, but other websites also can use that when they see your tiles.

Both the old (cc-by-sa) and the new (ODbL) license of the OSM data are only about the data itself. They say nothing about the software you use to work with this data. So the software can have any license you like.
