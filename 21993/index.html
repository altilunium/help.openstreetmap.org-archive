To give a general answer:

the OSM data is a kind of open data according to its licence. So you can do whatever you want with the OSM geodata as long as you do not break the licence.

And there are many opensource frameworks around that demonstrate how you can process the OSM data and how it is displayed etc.

Have an intensive look e.g. at the OSM wiki to find collections of websites and webservices dealing with OSM data in a special way.

BUT: "Sharing points of interest list to a specific group" is not a purpose of the OSM database or the main OSM website at osm.org
