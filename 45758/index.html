David, it helps when you give a particular polygon as below, you can click these if you switch on the data layer on the main website. It seems you refer to ways like this: http://www.openstreetmap.org/way/374132785 which is tagged natural=beach, surface=sand, and a bit of the beach rendering is lurking at the eastern side. It is also tagged type=multipolygon which only belongs on relations, not on ways.

This beach area is mostly overlapped with this way http://www.openstreetmap.org/way/146605238 tagged as riverbank.

The renderer paints the map in layers, so apparently the natural tag first, the waterway later, thus most of your beach gets covered by the water. Symbols and names follow on top.

Another of your beaches has a name, so you see 'North sands' named on the map, but only a tiny stripe lurking north-east.

The solution for you should probably be to separate (i.e. not overlap) the polygons and tag them individually.

Please also note that not everything that can be tagged is also rendered, for technical and style reasons.
