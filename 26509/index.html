Using housenumber interpolation is just a convenience for the mapper in a hurry. It's faster to map but less precise. In effect, what you're asking is "how much less precise can we tolerate to be ?". The answer to that question is personal, and could even differ from one editing session to the next.

Personally I'd make multiple ways, as precise as possible without being too tedious. Then again, the streets I map are usually short enough and often have housenumbers assigned a bit hapazardly, so I tend to not use interpolation at all.

After all this time, I came with a few simple rules for deciding when to make the address interpolation way cross or not cross other highways. These rules are:

  • If the last street number of the edge of the block described by the address interpolation way is known, then the address interpolation ends there.
  • If the last street number of the edge of the block described by the address interpolation way is not known, extend the way up the next known address number on the same side of the street, if it exists.
  • No address interpolation way would exist between two adjacent block's corners sharing the same co-linear edge if the address number of both corners is known

That would mean that the address interpolation way will cross other ways if one or both address numbers of adjacent blocks corners are not known (because in this case, there is the possibility of street numbers between the known corner and the non-corner street number) and will not cross other ways only if both of them are known (because, in this case, the street numbers between them are _known to be non-existent).

One image says a lot more than one thousand words, so:

  • The green arrows point known house numbers that are exactly on the block's corners
  • The red arrows show address interpolation ways that cross the adjacent streets because one or both of the house numbers of its block's corners are not known
  • The blue arrow show a house number that is not on the block's corner
  • The selected ways start and end inside the block's edge because its corner's house numbers are known.
